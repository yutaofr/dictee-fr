# Product Guidelines

## Prose Style & Tone
- **Tone:** Supportive and Teacher-like.
- **Voice:** Encouraging, clear, and empathetic. The application should feel like a patient mentor rather than a cold examiner.
- **Language:** Use inclusive and motivating French (e.g., "Bravo pour tes efforts !" instead of "Score : 12/20").

## Visual Identity
- **Theme:** Deep Blue Premium.
- **Aesthetic:** Utilize deep blue gradients, subtle glassmorphism (background-blur), and refined typography to create a high-end, focused atmosphere.
- **Typography:** Clear, legible sans-serif fonts with generous line height to reduce visual fatigue.

## User Experience (UX)
- **Primary Goal:** Deep Immersion.
- **Focus:** Once a dictation starts, the interface should minimize all non-essential elements. No notifications, no complex sidebars—just the text area and essential controls.
- **Navigation:** Simple, linear flow from text selection to dictation to correction.

## Correction & Feedback
- **Presentation:** Utilize categorized diffs with color-coded highlights to distinguish between spelling (lexical) and grammar (grammatical) errors.
- **Pedagogy:** Every error should be accompanied by a brief pedagogical note or a link to a French grammar rule, allowing the student to understand the *why* behind the correction.
- **Interactive Review:** Allow students to toggle between their original text and the corrected version to observe the changes clearly.

## Accessibility
- **Readability:** Ensure high contrast ratios within the deep blue theme.
- **Keyboard Support:** Full keyboard navigation for all actions (start, pause, re-read).
- **Audio Clarity:** The TTS voice must remain clear and audible over any background elements.
